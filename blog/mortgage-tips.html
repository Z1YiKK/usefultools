5 Proven Ways to Pay Off Your Mortgage 10 Years Early

📅 June 5, 2026  ·  ⏱️ 6 min read

Your mortgage is probably your biggest monthly expense — and your biggest opportunity to build wealth. By paying it off early, you can save tens or even hundreds of thousands in interest. Here are five proven strategies that actually work.

💰 Run the numbers: Use our Mortgage Payoff Calculator to see exactly how much YOU can save.

1. Make Bi-Weekly Payments (Instead of Monthly)

By paying half your mortgage every two weeks instead of the full amount once a month, you end up making 13 full payments per year instead of 12. On a $300,000 mortgage at 6.5%, this alone can shave 5-6 years off your loan term.

2. Round Up Your Payment

If your mortgage payment is $1,845, round it up to $2,000. That extra $155/month goes directly to principal. Over 30 years, this simple trick can save you $40,000+ in interest.

3. Apply Windfalls to Principal

Tax refunds, work bonuses, inheritance, or side hustle income — when you receive unexpected money, apply it to your mortgage principal. A single $5,000 lump-sum payment can knock 6-8 months off your loan.

4. Refinance Strategically (When Rates Drop)

Refinancing from a 6.5% to a 5.5% rate on a $300,000 loan saves about $200/month in interest. If you keep making the original payment amount, you're effectively making a $200/month extra principal payment — without feeling it in your budget.

5. The "One Extra Payment" Method

Make just one extra mortgage payment per year (apply it entirely to principal). On a standard 30-year loan, this alone can reduce your term by 4-5 years. Split it into 12 small additions each month and you won't even notice it.
