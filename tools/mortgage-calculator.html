How Extra Payments Save You Money

Making extra payments toward your mortgage principal is one of the smartest financial moves you can make. Even a small additional amount each month can cut years off your loan and save tens of thousands in interest.

Why It Works

Mortgage interest is calculated on your remaining balance. Every extra dollar you pay goes directly to reducing the principal, which means less interest accrues next month — and the savings compound over time.

Example: On a $300,000 mortgage at 6.5%, adding just $200/month saves approximately $97,000 in interest and pays off the loan 7 years early.
