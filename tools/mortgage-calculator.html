How Extra Mortgage Payments Work

Every extra dollar you pay goes directly to reducing the principal balance, not interest. This means less interest accrues in future months — creating a compounding savings effect.

Example: On a $300,000 mortgage at 6.5%, adding $200/month to principal saves approximately $97,000 in interest and pays off the loan 7 years early.

Pro Tips

  • Bi-weekly payments: Pay half your mortgage every 2 weeks → 13 full payments per year instead of 12 → 5+ years saved.
  • Round up: If your payment is $1,847, round to $2,000. The extra $153/month is painless but powerful.
  • Windfalls: Apply tax refunds or bonuses as one-time principal payments. Even $5,000 once can knock months off your loan.
  • Refinance check: If rates drop 1%+ below your current rate, refinancing may save more than extra payments. Run both scenarios.
