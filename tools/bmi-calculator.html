What BMI Tells You

BMI = weight ÷ height². It was developed by Adolphe Quetelet in the 1830s as a population-level statistical tool, now widely used as a health screening measure.

BMI Categories (WHO)

  • Below 18.5: Underweight
  • 18.5–24.9: Normal weight
  • 25.0–29.9: Overweight
  • 30.0–34.9: Obesity Class I
  • 35.0–39.9: Obesity Class II
  • 40.0+: Obesity Class III

Limitations: BMI does not distinguish muscle from fat, consider body composition and distribution. Athletes, elderly individuals, and certain ethnic groups may need adjusted interpretations. (WHO, 2024; AMA, 2023)
