How the Calorie Calculator Works

This calculator uses the Mifflin-St Jeor equation (1990), considered the most accurate BMR formula for the general population by the Academy of Nutrition and Dietetics:

Male: BMR = 10 x weight(kg) + 6.25 x height(cm) - 5 x age + 5

Female: BMR = 10 x weight(kg) + 6.25 x height(cm) - 5 x age - 161

BMR vs TDEE

BMR (Basal Metabolic Rate) = calories burned at complete rest — just breathing and keeping your organs running. TDEE (Total Daily Energy Expenditure) = BMR x activity multiplier — your actual daily burn including movement.

Important Notes

  • These are estimates. Individual variation exists due to genetics, muscle mass, metabolic health, and hormones (ACSM, 2022).
  • Weight loss deficits below BMR are not recommended without medical supervision (NIH guidelines).
  • Macro split shown is a general recommendation (30% protein, 30% fat, 40% carbs). Adjust based on your diet approach.
  • For athletes or clinical needs, consult a registered dietitian.
