Tipping Customs Worldwide

Tipping norms vary dramatically by country. In the US, 15-20% is standard due to the tip credit wage system ($2.13/hr federal minimum for tipped workers). In Japan, tipping is considered rude. In much of Europe, a service charge is included in the bill.

  • US: 15-20%, servers rely on tips for income
  • UK: 10-12.5% often included as "service charge"
  • EU: 5-10%, round up for good service
  • Japan: 0%, tipping can be offensive
  • Australia: 0-5%, high minimum wage reduces reliance on tips
